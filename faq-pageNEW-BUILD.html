Shipping & Turnaround Time

What shipping company do you use?
We use multiple shipping carriers including FedEx, R&L Carriers, and USPS. We will choose the best carrier for your order, depending on the product and the shipping class chosen. Due to the size of our items, there are NO expedited shipping options available.

What is the difference between Economy & Premium shipping?

The premium bumper will come with a limited warranty of ten years on the paint job itself. It will also arrive with an extra application of clear coat. Aside from not being folded for shipping purposes, these are the perks for purchasing the premium option. The economy version has a 6 month warranty on the paint job itself. Since the bumper is shipped with the ends folded inward, the part will ship for FREE.

How can you ship a bumper cover folded?

Folding a bumper will not change the integrity of the bumper itself. In fact, folding a bumper is becoming common practice for all online vendors. Our company only folds the ends of the bumper inward, this makes the package smaller and more cost friendly to ship. Plastic is very pliable so once a folded bumper is received, you just need to set it outside to allow the plastic to expand back to its normal shape.

Our Painting Process

What kind of paint do you use?
We use one of the worlds leading OEM automotive coatings on everything that we paint.

What is the painting process?
The paint job is of the highest quality; we follow OEM specifications for painting. We paint all our parts in our new state-of-the-art paint booth which greatly enhances our efficiency overall. We apply 3 coats of base color and then they are coated with 2 coats of clear coat, before it goes into the baking tunnel. The parts then pass through our quality control inspection before they are shipped out of our facilities.

Why do you put powder on the part before you ship it out?

The powder on the bumper is corn starch and we use that to protect the fresh paint on the part from getting friction scratches in transit. Please use a soft dry cloth or pure water to remove the majority of the powder. Then you can use a damp soft cloth to remove the remaining powder.

How long should I wait to power wash/wax my car?
Please do not do any type of pressure washing or waxing on this part for at least 30 days. The paint will still be fairly soft and does need that amount of time to fully cure.
